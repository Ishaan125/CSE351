1) Boundary Tag Size: 8 bytes  
   Reasoning: For malloc sizes 1 through 24, the difference between the two addresses is always 0x20 = 32 bytes, meaning all those payloads fit in a 32-byte block. When the size increases to 25, the difference jumps to 0x30 = 48 bytes. This means a 24-byte payload fits in a 32-byte block but a 25-byte payload does not, so the overhead must be exactly 32 - 24 = 8 bytes.
